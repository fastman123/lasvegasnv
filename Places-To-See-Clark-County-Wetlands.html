Clark County Wetlands

Clark county wetlands park, sandwiched between Las Vegas and Lake Mead about a mile east of Boulder Highway and Tropicana Avenue. Clark county wetlands park is the ultimate place where visitors can escape that hassle of the city and get immersed in a beautiful oasis of tall trees and some fresh babbling streams. The facility which covers an entire 2900 acres has proven itself to be one of Las Vegas best-kept secret for nature lovers and people who fancy a good time.


 

The Nature preserve is the centerpiece of the park featuring a range of wildlife, site seeing, educational signage and an outdoor amphitheater. It covers an area of 210 acres, this reserve is a distinct habitat for wildlife and trees alike. Visitors get to walk through paved and unpaved trail while exploring and discovering nature like never before.

On your visit make sure you bring your camera along and experience nature unfolding. You will also get the chance to perfectly view and take that perfect shot of birds and other wildlife. The park also has some cool shades and some seating areas where you can always cool your heels.

With close to 120 bird species and more than 70 species of mammals and reptiles, the park also boasts of some 13 miles of hiking trails. With this, you get the chance to follow the trails along the facility's streams while keeping your eye out for some ponds and plants among them some cottonwood trees and some willows.

 

No pets are allowed in the Nature Preserve, however, the Duck Creek trail which is located in Broadbent Boulevard do permit pets as long as they are on a leash. Picnicking in the nature preserve is only allowed at the picnic café in the nature center.

Popular sites in the Nature Preserve

Boardwalk pond- this is among the best spots where you get to see wildlife in the park, here you also get to bask in the wonderful waters in the desert. You will spot turtles, fish and birds in the pond but remember not to feed them.

North pond wildlife blind

This pond features a wildlife blind overlooking another pond where visitors can catch a glimpse of the great birds in the preserve.

Cottonwood grove

Get to discover the calm serenity of nature as you experience the transformation of the groves during spring where cottony seeds blow around, while during autumn the trees are awash in yellow and gold.

Vern's pond and island

Consisting of some shade structure and several benches the spot offers you some great chance to sit back and reflect on nature's best.

Upper division Weir Bridge

The big bridge spanning the Las Vegas Wash is the best spot to view the Valley's urban river were close to 180 million gallons of water pass daily.
